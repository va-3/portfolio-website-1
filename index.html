About Me

I'm driven by understanding people and building solutions that matter. As golf team captain in high school, I learned to lead by example: showing up early, staying late, and holding myself to the standard I set for my team. Early service jobs taught me that real outcomes start with listening, whether it's a regular asking about their day or a nervous parent needing reassurance their child would be safe. Sports taught me to show up and do the work. Those jobs taught me why the work matters.

At UT Dallas studying Computer Information Systems, I led Pi Kappa Phi's digital transformation as Historian, architecting our first alumni network to strengthen connections and drive meaningful philanthropy impact. At Vivint Smart Home, I transitioned from technical sales to development when I realized I wanted to expand beyond presenting solutions. I wanted to build them. I dove into development, exploring how technology could solve real problems across industries. Mental health technology stood out as where code could create genuine impact.

Today, as a Full Stack Engineer at PeerBridge, I lead cross-functional teams alongside designers, clinicians, and product managers to build AI-powered solutions in mental health technology. Beyond work, I dive into side projects across fintech, education, and emerging tech spaces because I genuinely love exploring how technology intersects with business problems. Every code review, every new framework, every conversation with domain experts teaches me something. I approach building the future the same way I approached leading my golf team and supporting my brothers: with curiosity, humility, and a commitment to learning from the people around me.
